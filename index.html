How to read this site

Facts and interpretation stay separate.

F

Documented fact

What an audit report, council record, or evidence-database entry directly states.

I

Supported interpretation

A careful explanation based on the documented pattern. It is not an auditor conclusion.

?

Open question

Something the available records do not yet answer.

About this project

What this tool does—and does not do

This site helps Lincoln residents understand the City's audits and the public records related to them using plain-language explanations, timelines, and topic guides.

Built from public records

Every public statement should connect to the public records, a curated database built from publicly available records. The database is the authoritative source used by this application.

Review the original public records

You are encouraged to review the original public records, including independent audit reports, City Council agendas, meeting packets, approved minutes, ordinances, financial reports, and video recordings of City Council meetings available on the City's public website.

Project independence

Lincoln Audit Explorer is an independent public information project. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or approved by the City of Lincoln or any government agency.

Important limitation

Council minutes can document discussion and action, but they do not automatically prove that an audit finding was corrected or that a control worked. Later audit testing is stronger evidence of effectiveness.

Reading labels

Documented fact means the record directly supports the statement. Supported interpretation means the conclusion is based on documented evidence but is not itself an auditor’s conclusion.
